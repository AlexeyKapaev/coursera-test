Fondue

A national dish of Swiss cuisine, also common in the regions of France and Italy bordering Switzerland. It is prepared from a mixture of various Swiss cheeses with the addition of white wine, starch (for thickening) and kirsch; as an option, you can add garlic, nutmeg, etc.
It is prepared on an open fire in a special heat-resistant dish called a caquelon (fr. caquelon) and is usually used in a company: during a meal, pieces of bread, potatoes, gherkins, olives and other products planted on special long forks are dipped in the resulting mass.

Ratatouille

A classic vegetable dish of Provencal cuisine made from peppers, eggplant and zucchini, in many respects likely to be a Hungarian lecho. The first ratatouille recipe is found in a 1778 cookbook. Ratatouille, which arose in the area of ​​​​modern Nice, was a pest of peasants who were poor in the summer, who prepared it from fresh vegetables. In the original recipe visit zucchini, tomatoes, peppers, onions and garlic. In the modern version of the dishes, eggplant is also added.

Coq au vin

Classic French chicken dish. In France, there are a large number of recipes for stewing chicken in wine, and each wine region has at least one of its own. Recipes differ not only in the name, which is given in accordance with the wine used in cooking, but also in the corresponding aroma. It is believed that the birthplace of this dish is Burgundy, so the rooster in Burgundy wine is considered a classic recipe for coq-au-veins. Cock-au-ven is usually served with a baguette.
